## Salience Nucleus (Invariant Delta)

> As self-trust and insight increase, the baseline sense of ongoing threat decreases because present safety is better modeled; residual anxiety reflects awareness of abstract, non-immediate past threats rather than current danger.

---

## Purpose

This artifact formalizes why deep insight and self-trust growth are associated with a **net decrease in chronic threat**, even when anxiety intermittently appears during reflection or reconsolidation.

It clarifies the difference between **present danger** and **historical threat awareness**.

---

## Core Principle

The nervous system differentiates between:
- **immediate survival threat**
- **abstract knowledge of past vulnerability**

As self-trust improves, the system becomes better at separating the two.

---

## Mechanism Overview

### 1. Self-Trust as a Threat-Reduction Variable

Self-trust increases when:
- survival calculus becomes accurate
- past misattributions are corrected
- agency is restored
- prediction improves

As a result:
- fewer situations are classified as dangerous
- baseline vigilance decreases
- ongoing threat load drops

This effect is cumulative.

---

### 2. Why Anxiety Can Still Appear

Residual anxiety may surface when:
- past high-risk states are cognitively accessed
- vulnerability is recognized retroactively
- abstract awareness of former danger becomes salient

This anxiety is:
- informational, not reactive
- memory-linked, not environment-linked
- non-immediate and non-directive

It does **not** require action.

---

### 3. Distinguishing Current Threat from Historical Awareness

The critical distinction is:

- **Current threat** → demands response  
- **Past threat awareness** → demands context  

When self-trust is high, the system can correctly classify:
> “This feeling is about what *was*, not what *is*.”

This classification prevents escalation.

---

## Why Overall Threat Still Decreases

Even with intermittent anxiety:
- the nervous system spends less time mobilized
- fewer loops remain unresolved
- vigilance is no longer default
- emotional spikes resolve quickly

The net trajectory is downward threat load.

---

## Long-Term Integration Effect

With repeated correct classification:
- anxiety spikes become milder
- memories lose urgency
- compassion remains without fear
- self-image stabilizes

The system learns:
> “Awareness does not equal danger.”

---

## High-Compression Summary

Growing self-trust lowers ongoing threat because present safety is modeled accurately; remaining anxiety reflects abstract awareness of past danger rather than current survival risk.
