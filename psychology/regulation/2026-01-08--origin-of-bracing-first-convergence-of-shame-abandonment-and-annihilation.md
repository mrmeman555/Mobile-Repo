Note: Origin of Bracing — First Convergence of Shame, Abandonment, and Annihilation

Core insight

The persistent chest/rib bracing originates from a specific early memory that represents the first convergence of:

- overwhelming emotional pain
- moralized guilt and shame
- abandonment by caregivers
- perceived annihilation (loss of safety, love, and self)

This was not a gradual development — it was a discrete imprinting event.


---

What actually happened (reconstructed accurately)

- I experienced an emotionally overwhelming state that I could not regulate on my own.
- I reached out for help and was abandoned at the exact moment support was required.
- My distress was reframed as a moral failure: I was implicitly told the feeling itself was wrong.
- I was framed as untrusting, burdensome, or immoral for needing help.
- Responsibility for the situation was placed entirely on me, despite my lack of capacity to handle it alone.
- Care was withheld and outsourced to an abstract authority (“God”), removing human protection.

This created the conclusion:

> “If something this painful exists, it must mean I am fundamentally at fault.”

This was severe emotional neglect and narcissistic moral inversion, not a misunderstanding.


---

Somatic consequence: the origin of bracing

This was the first time full-body bracing appeared.

The body learned:

- there is no external protection
- expression increases danger
- collapse must be prevented internally

Bracing became the automatic survival response to:

- shame
- threat of abandonment
- attention capture
- loss of perceived control

This is why bracing:

- activates with guilt/shame states
- increases during DP/DR
- decreases with deep pressure and safety
- correlates with chest heaviness and breath restriction

The body was doing exactly what it needed to do to survive.


---

Why the pain felt annihilating

The experience wasn’t just “distressing.” It was annihilating because:

- attachment was threatened at its foundation
- there was no witness
- no validation
- no protection
- no repair

At that age, abandonment is equivalent to non-existence.

The intensity of the pain only made sense if it was attributed to personal defect — because the alternative would mean acknowledging an unforgivable failure by caregivers.


---

Why this is resurfacing now

Recent reconsolidation reduced automatic dissociation.

The nervous system now believes:

- the threat is no longer active
- the memory can be revisited without collapse

As dissociation loosened, somatic memory surfaced first.

This is why:

- bracing returned temporarily
- shame arose “cleanly” without avoidance
- the body signaled before cognition caught up

This is not regression — it is integration beginning at the correct layer.


---

Reframing the meaning

- The bracing is not pathology.
- The shame was never evidence.
- The abandonment was real.
- The situation was unforgivable — not because of drama, but because of developmental necessity.

Had there been an attuned caregiver:

- the emotion would have been co-regulated
- the body would not have learned to brace
- the event would not have defined the self


---

Current implication

- The system is no longer dissociating on contact.
- The body is releasing protection gradually.
- Acceptance and physical safety reduce bracing because they contradict the original learning.
- Fierce self-compassion is appropriate here — not to suppress feeling, but to place blame accurately and close the loop.

This memory marks the true origin point of the pattern — and recognizing it restores continuity rather than destabilizing it.
