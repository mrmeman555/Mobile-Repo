# Event Embedding — Unsolicited Green Juice Interaction

## Phase A — Event Embedding (Immediate, Lossless Capture)

### A1. Core Event Representation (High-Resolution)

#### 1. Observable Facts

- Location: Kitchen
- Participants: Father, me
- Temporal context: Occurred shortly after a prior conflict involving authority, explanation avoidance, and boundary assertion.

**Sequence of events:**

1. I entered the kitchen.
2. Father said: “Hey, I need you to try this.”
3. I asked: “What is it?”
4. Father did not answer the question.
5. Father walked to the refrigerator and retrieved a mason jar containing a frothy green liquid.
6. Father retrieved a cup.
7. Approximately 7–10 seconds elapsed from my question without an answer.
8. During this time, father began explaining how I should think about the drink and that it was something I “need,” without stating what it was or why I would need it.
9. Father moved quickly toward me and began to pour the liquid into the cup.
10. Just before the pour was completed, I interrupted and said: “No thank you. I will look into it.”
11. Father stopped and agreed.

No raised voices. No explicit argument. Interaction ended immediately after refusal.

- I chose to interrupt before physical completion to avoid post-commitment pressure.
