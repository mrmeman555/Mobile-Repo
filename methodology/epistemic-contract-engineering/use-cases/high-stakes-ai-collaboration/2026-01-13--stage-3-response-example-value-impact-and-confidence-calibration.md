## 1. Decomposition of the Work

### A. Self-Authorship Control Architecture

**Problem targeted:**  
Chronic identity instability (DP/DR, shame-driven collapse, compulsive self-regulation) where individuals cannot reliably select or sustain a coherent self-model.

**Why existing approaches fragment:**  
Most therapies treat identity as emergent or narrative-only, not as a controllable system variable. Techniques are siloed (CBT, EMDR, parts work) without an explicit authority-selection mechanism.

**New control leverage introduced:**  
A direct mechanism for assigning authority to a chosen self-model, with explicit refusal of illegitimate frames. This creates volitional identity stability rather than passive coherence.

---

### B. Reconsolidation via Chosen Belief and Identity Authority

**Problem targeted:**  
Memories and self-representations re-trigger harm because their meaning is passively reactivated rather than re-authored.

**Why existing approaches fragment:**  
Reconsolidation is often treated as stimulus-based (EMDR, exposure) without accounting for the belief authority governing interpretation.

**New control leverage introduced:**  
A method for actively selecting the interpretive authority during reconsolidation, allowing meaning to be rewritten at the identity layer rather than merely dampened.

---

### C. Functional Model of Narcissism as Shame Regulation

**Problem targeted:**  
Misclassification of narcissism as character pathology rather than a regulatory strategy, leading to ineffective treatment and moralization.

**Why existing approaches fragment:**  
Trait-based or moralized frameworks obscure function and fail to explain overlap with dissociation, dominance behavior, and collapse.

**New control leverage introduced:**  
A function-first model: narcissism as a self-stabilization response to unbearable shame, integrated cleanly with dominance, scapegoating, and identity control.

---

### D. Bottom-Up Stabilization via Eye-Gaze / Attention Control

**Problem targeted:**  
Top-down cognitive techniques fail when the nervous system is too dysregulated to permit narrative or belief-level intervention.

**Why existing approaches fragment:**  
Somatic and cognitive methods are often split, with no integration into identity reconsolidation.

**New control leverage introduced:**  
A bottom-up attentional control channel that reliably stabilizes state, enabling higher-order identity work to occur.
