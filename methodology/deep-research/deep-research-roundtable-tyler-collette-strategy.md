## Core Asset: The Deep Research Roundtable Method
The deep research roundtable functions as a research acceleration engine. Its value is not insight generation alone, but rapid conversion of insight into defensible, researcher‑legible artifacts.

This is the dividing line between “insight” and publishable research.

## Research Packet Concept
The goal is to produce a low‑friction research packet that a serious researcher can evaluate quickly.

### A Strong Packet Contains Five Components

#### A) One‑Page Model Summary (Thesis)
- Clear definition of the LK mechanism (in distilled, technical language)
- What it does: regulation + self‑model stabilization
- Distinction from adjacent constructs
- Placement within existing therapeutic families

#### B) Concept‑to‑Construct Translation Table
Columns:

- Your term (e.g., “proximity‑encoded benevolent presence”)
- Closest academic constructs
- Overlap
- Distinction
- Measurement ideas

#### C) Predictions (Three Tiers)
- Phenomenological: subjective reports
- Behavioral: avoidance reduction, persistence, task initiation latency
- Physiological: tremor release, ocular changes, HRV, startle response

#### D) Minimal Experiment Designs (2–3)
- One low‑cost
- One medium
- One high‑rigor

Key manipulations include:

- LK vs neutral imagery vs self‑talk
- Proximity manipulation (near vs abstract presence)

#### E) Annotated Bibliography
- 10–20 load‑bearing sources
- Each with 1–2 lines on relevance

## How to Run the Roundtable to Produce This Packet

### Assigned Roles
1. Primary Theorist – clean articulation of the model
2. Literature Cartographer – mapping to existing constructs
3. Operationalizer – variables and measures
4. Skeptical Reviewer – forces falsifiability and clarity
5. Experimental Designer – study designs
6. Clinical Translator – links to therapy and applied value

### Constraints
- No poetic or metaphorical language
- Every core claim must specify:
  - Mechanism
  - Prediction
  - Test
- Separate lived experience from theoretical assertion

These constraints are what make the output academically credible.
