# Personal Suffering Narrative (Acute) — Draft 1

## 1) Marriage / Life-Building → Coerced Separation → Narrative Rewriting
You built a real, functional life and trajectory with your ex-wife for years. Then you were relentlessly pressured (especially by your father) to leave her. After you did, the entire reality was rewritten: instead of recognizing the years of effort and the coercive pressure, it was reframed as you “failing,” being morally implicated, and needing to be “rescued.”
So a long period of competence and stability became retroactively converted into a story of defect and collapse—despite the fact you had been building and sustaining something real.

---

## 2) 20+ Years of Religious Striving → Collapse of the Framework
For over two decades you strived relentlessly to know God—leading Bible studies, studying continuously, doing everything you could to “get relief” and spiritual security. The persistent theme was: if you weren’t getting relief, it must be your fault.
You carried that burden for years—until you eventually reached a breaking point where you realized it was completely illogical that a God existed who provides provision, because your life could not plausibly be unfolding the way it was if that model were true.
So you didn’t just “lose faith”—you endured an extended, high-investment pursuit that ended in a severe epistemic rupture: the entire frame you’d used to interpret suffering and safety collapsed.

---

## 3) 2016 Clarity + “First Real Self” → Business Pursuit → Full Regression
In 2016 you had that period of clarity you’ve referenced before—deep, hard-to-explain growth. Following that, you had a successful summer with your business, where you felt like yourself for the first time in your life. It felt like you had finally healed.

You then made a high-stakes decision: you paused school to pursue your goals and push business full-time.
And then—despite your absolute best efforts—you experienced complete regression. You struggled in business for several years, trying to understand what was happening internally, until you became unable to continue, later understanding this was driven by undiagnosed OCD and CPTSD (and the dissociation you didn’t yet have a framework for).

So the sequence was: breakthrough → competence → courageous commitment → unexplained internal collapse → years of grinding without a correct model.

---

## 4) Massive Financial Sacrifice → Prolonged Survival Grinding → Late Return to School
You took out student loans to pay for your wife’s master’s degree. After that, you spent several more years in survival mode—trying to pay bills while being essentially incapacitated by invisible mental health issues—still relentlessly searching for answers, even though by that point you had already dedicated about eight years to nonstop self-analysis and investigation.

Then your wife completed her master’s degree, you returned to school, and you two moved to Austin. You were finally able to support yourselves more effectively. You decided to finish your psych degree.

During this period you were grinding on multiple fronts:

- pursuing academics intensely
- carrying the reality of OCD (once you had that label)
- diving into OCD and mindfulness content relentlessly
- reading essentially everything you could find
- working out relentlessly (half marathons, etc.)
- meditating for hours for years

So it wasn’t “trying.” It was sustained, disciplined, multi-year effort.

---

## 5) Near-PhD Track Opportunity → “Technicality” Failure
After graduating psych with a 3.98, you got a single opportunity to earn the experience you needed to pursue a PhD. That path did not work out, and you describe it as failing due to a technicality.
So you had the competence, the grades, the trajectory, and then the ladder rung snapped—not due to lack of effort or ability.

Meanwhile, throughout this whole arc, your parents remained emotionally detached and continued scapegoating you.

---

## 6) Graduation + COVID → Strategic Pivot to Tech → High-Level Independent Execution
After graduation, COVID hit, and you had to choose direction. You chose tech.

You executed a high-difficulty pivot with unusually strong self-directed performance:

- completed Harvard CS50 completely independently without looking at reference sources
- retaught yourself all math prerequisites up through Calculus I
- enrolled in a comp-sci degree path at UTSA
- took Programming I, Programming II, and Calc I
- got A’s in all of them

So while still carrying heavy internal strain, you built a new competence stack under pressure.

---

## 7) Marriage Deterioration Under Abuse + Sexual Withholding → Collapse of Advanced Plans
During this time, your wife took zero accountability, and you describe her as narcissistically abusive. Sexual connection was absent (vaginismus, and the reality that you weren’t being met relationally).

You were encouraged into a master’s in computer science, but it flopped because the environment collapsed: you had to pursue divorce because she didn’t respect you, and your father was simultaneously pressuring you to leave.
So your plan didn’t fail in a vacuum—it failed inside a relational + family coercion storm.

---

## 8) Post-Separation: Injury + Betrayal Disclosure + Family Abuse Escalation
After leaving your wife, your father immediately began abusing you and treating you like a burden.

Then you broke your ribs on your bike.

Then your wife told you that she had lost her virginity to someone you knew.

And throughout all of this, you still had no correct framework for narcissism or CPTSD, and remained plagued by constant dissociation—compulsive, involuntary, and disabling.

---

## 9) Where This Leaves the Narrative
You’re clear this is not the full story—there’s more. But this is the starting point: a sequence of high-investment striving, repeated destabilization, coercion and rewriting, major sacrifices, intermittent breakthroughs, and repeated collapses caused not by laziness or lack of ability, but by:

- missing diagnostic frameworks at the time
- sustained coercion / narrative reversal
- relational abuse
- family scapegoating and destabilization
- physical injury
- existential belief collapse (God framework)

---

## Quick Check: The “Core Pattern” This Draft Makes Visible
You build and sustain real competence and trajectories.

External systems repeatedly apply coercion, rewriting, and destabilization.

You keep investing anyway—often with extreme discipline.

When things collapse, the story is rewritten as your deficiency rather than the actual causal structure.

You continue, re-tool, pivot, rebuild—and now you’re finally assembling the missing model that explains why the engine kept stalling.
