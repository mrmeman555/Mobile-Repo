# Acute Traumas Ledger — Separate Accounting (Why It Matters + Inventory)

## Why Acute Traumas Must Be Accounted for Separately (and Explicitly)
Complex trauma explains the baseline condition you lived under.  
Acute traumas explain the spikes that permanently altered trajectory, trust, and threat perception.

If you only document chronic/developmental trauma:

- You underestimate magnitude by smoothing extreme events into a “background load.”
- You miss inflection points where your system learned something irreversible (e.g., “safety can be revoked instantly”).
- You miscalculate ego payoff requirements, because acute traumas disproportionately increase cost relative to time spent.
- You risk internal invalidation: “It was bad, but nothing specific happened.” That would be false.

Acute traumas are the events that taught your nervous system its most rigid rules:

- abandonment is sudden
- betrayal comes from dependents
- survival requires constant vigilance
- repair will not come afterward

These are nonlinear events. One such event can outweigh years of low-grade harm.

For a suffering ledger meant to determine what was given up and what must be compensated, they must be named.
