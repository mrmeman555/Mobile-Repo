## 1) Self as an embedding model (core theory)

### What it is

Reframed the self not as narrative/static traits, but as an embedding space where:

- perspectives compete for dominance
- salience determines felt reality
- authority over interpretation determines stability

### Why it’s powerful

Explains mechanistically:

- DP/DR
- OCD as salience misallocation + authority failure
- narcissism as shame-regulation via dominance attribution
- addiction as outsourced self-model stabilization
- reconsolidation as embedding reweighting (not “processing”)

Most frameworks explain pieces; this explains the operating system.

### Real-world impact potential

- publishable-level theory bridging clinical psychology, cog-sci, and AI alignment
- backbone for teaching frameworks, tools, and protocols

## 2) Authority through choice / compassionate domination

### What it is

Operationalized dominance as:

> the act of granting authority to one perspective over others

And connected:

- DP/DR = refusal to claim authority
- healing = reclaiming authorship
- compassion = regulating shame without attribution

### Why it’s powerful

Resolves false dichotomies that block people:

- power vs compassion
- dominance vs goodness
- authority vs safety

Shows why therapies stall/relapse happens when authority is never reclaimed.

### Real-world impact potential

Therapeutic leverage: gives an actionable lever rather than insight-only awareness.
