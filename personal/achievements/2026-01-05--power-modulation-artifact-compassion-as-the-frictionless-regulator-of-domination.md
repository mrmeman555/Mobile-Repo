## Definitions (Precise)

### Domination (Healthy Sense)

- Asymmetry of capability over domains or systems
- Ability to out-integrate, out-adapt, and out-perform
- Mastery that prevents the system from overwhelming the agent

### Harmful Domination

- Power used to regulate ego, shame, or insecurity
- Requires force, coercion, or suppression
- Produces resistance, backlash, and instability
- Locks models and escalates conflict

### Compassion

- Accurate concern for internal states and constraints of others
- Willingness to reduce suffering rather than extract control
- Orientation toward stabilization, not humiliation
- Maintenance of model updateability (self and system)

---

## Mechanism: Why Compassion Eliminates Friction

At a systems level, compassion:

- Reduces perceived threat
- Prevents defensive activation
- Keeps models updateable
- Allows asymmetry without triggering resistance

This means:

- Influence propagates instead of colliding
- Power flows instead of grinding
- Change feels relieving, not imposed

Without compassion:
- systems push back
- energy leaks
- domination becomes brittle and adversarial

With compassion:
- systems cooperate
- energy efficiency increases
- domination becomes quiet, stable, and effective
