## Purpose

This note exists to explicitly acknowledge the **upper bound of realistic impact** of the work, because failing to name that upper bound keeps the safety and meaning loop open.

This is not a claim of certainty or inevitability.
It is an honest accounting of **what is possible if the mechanisms are learned and applied**.

---

## The Core Claim (Unhedged)

The ideas and mechanisms developed are not niche, aesthetic, or idiosyncratic.

They target:
- threat-driven identity instability
- chronic internal vigilance
- model freezing via anger and ego defense
- loss of groundedness under uncertainty
- premature convergence of self-models

These conditions are **widespread**, cross-cultural, and developmentally common.

Therefore:

> **If these mechanisms are understood and applied, they can meaningfully reduce suffering for a very large portion of people who encounter these threat patterns.**

---

## Why the Impact Is Broad (Mechanistic Basis)

The mechanisms operate at the level of:
- learning rate under threat
- confidence-weighting of belief
- self-model plasticity
- update policies (anger vs compassion)
- epistemic trust in perception

These are **foundational layers** of human cognition.

They are not:
- diagnosis-specific
- trauma-type-specific
- culture-specific
- personality-specific

They apply wherever:
- humans protect identity through certainty
- threat drives rigidity
- suffering persists due to frozen models

This describes **a substantial fraction of the human population**.

## Realistic Upper Bound of Impact (Explicit)

At the upper bound:

- Anyone capable of reflective learning
- Who experiences chronic threat or vigilance
- And who can adopt update-preserving strategies

…can benefit.

This includes:
- people with trauma histories
- people stuck in anger or bitterness
- people with identity diffusion or dissociation
- people caught in narcissistic or defensive loops
- people unable to remain grounded under stress

The ceiling is not “a few”.
The ceiling is **many**.
