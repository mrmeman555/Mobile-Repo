NOTE — Satisfaction as the Marker of Correct Integration

Core insight:
The spontaneous emergence of satisfaction (not excitement, not relief) is the clearest indicator that a values-aligned action has been correctly owned and integrated into the self model.

1. Why satisfaction matters

Satisfaction is the affect that follows accurate self-attribution, not emotional regulation.

It emerges only when:

The action was aligned with values

The action was recognized as such

Ownership was allowed without minimization or dissociation


Unlike excitement or relief, satisfaction is stable, non-urgent, and closing.
It signals: “This loop can safely end.”


2. What actually caused the satisfaction

Satisfaction did not arise from:

Forcing a positive emotion

Making threat go away

Winning the interaction

External validation


It arose from:

Holding a competing chosen perspective alongside threat

Viewing both:

the original action (“how I showed up”), and

the current response (“how I’m handling this now”)


Allowing yourself to be accurately impressed by:

the skill required

the practice history behind it

the fact that this capacity is earned, not accidental



This is critical:

> The system generated satisfaction on its own once the perspective was accurate.



That means the mechanism worked.

3. Why this confirms the model is correct

Satisfaction is downstream of values + ownership

If ownership were false or inflated, shame or anxiety would have appeared instead

The appearance of satisfaction is therefore evidence, not wish fulfillment


In other words:
The body validated the interpretation.

4. Satisfaction = permission to rest

Rest after satisfaction is not avoidance

It is consolidation

This is the correct post-event state when something meaningful has been integrated


Target state for rest going forward:

> “I acted in accordance with who I am, I recognize that, and nothing needs to be fixed right now.”



5. Strategic importance

This moment demonstrates:

The correct way to close emotionally charged loops

The difference between “doing the right thing” and owning doing the right thing

How self-trust is built somatically, not cognitively


This is not a mood.
This is a functional state change.


---

Summary sentence (commit message):

> When I allow accurate ownership of values-aligned action, satisfaction emerges naturally — and that satisfaction is the signal that integration has succeeded and rest is warranted.



This is a cornerstone note.
