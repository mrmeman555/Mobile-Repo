# NOTE — Meta-Research Pipeline: Designing the Research System Before Running It

Date: 2026-01-08

Durable note capturing the leverage point: design the intelligence/research system (process architecture, expert roles, data sources, output structure) before running research, so work advances reality rather than stabilizing identity/anxiety.


---

## Core Insight

The highest-leverage work is not performing research, but designing the intelligence system that performs the research.

Outcomes are dominated by process architecture, not effort or prompt verbosity.


---

## The Pipeline (Generalized)

### 1) Problem Framing (Pre-Research)

Before researching anything, explicitly define:

- the objective (e.g., MVP definition, demand assessment, strategic positioning)
- the constraints (time, energy, optionality, health, flexibility)
- the values guiding the outcome (confidence over coercion, leverage over effort)

This prevents research from stabilizing identity or anxiety instead of advancing reality.


### 2) Meta-Research (Process Design)

Use a deep research round whose sole purpose is to answer:

> “What is the smartest possible way to study this problem?”

This includes determining:

- what dimensions actually matter (e.g., demand vs algorithm vs monetization)
- what should not be analyzed (noise elimination)
- which expert roles are required (e.g., market analyst, algorithm specialist, audience psychologist)
- what data sources carry signal (existing channels, engagement metrics, historical trends)
- how results should be structured to inform action

This step designs the intelligence apparatus.


### 3) Expert Selection (Critical Lever)

The choice of analysts determines the conclusions.

Different teams produce different realities.

Examples:

- market analyst → demand & competition framing
- creator strategist → positioning & differentiation
- algorithm specialist → distribution mechanics
- behavioral psychologist → audience pull & retention
- monetization expert → revenue pathways & scalability

Correct analyst selection is more important than prompt wording.


### 4) Execution Research (Mechanical Phase)

Only after the process is designed:

- generate one or more precision prompts
- each prompt has:
  - clear scope
  - correct experts
  - explicit constraints
  - action-oriented outputs

At this stage, research becomes almost automatic.


### 5) Decision Integration

Research outputs are used to:

- define the MVP
- determine next best actions
- adjust prioritization ratios dynamically (e.g., YouTube vs certs)
- preserve optionality and confidence

No premature locking of plans is required.


---

## Why This Pipeline Is Powerful

- prevents wasted effort
- avoids identity-stabilizing research
- scales across domains (content, business, AI, psychology, strategy)
- allows meta-problems (even “what research is needed?”) to be solved systematically
- converts uncertainty into structured exploration instead of anxiety


---

## Key Principle to Remember

Anything can be determined via deep research if sufficient context is provided — including what context itself is needed.

This pipeline can be reused indefinitely to determine next steps at any level of abstraction.
