## Salience Nucleus (Invariant Delta)

> Rapid insight cascades occur when a newly accurate model begins resolving accumulated survival-calculus debt, causing the brain to aggressively reprocess past, present, and anticipated data to correct misattributions and reduce long-standing threat load, producing sustained relief rather than instability.

---

## Purpose

This artifact formalizes why insight generation can suddenly accelerate, feel compulsive yet relieving, and produce a durable upward trajectory rather than emotional volatility.

It explains this phase as **debt repayment**, not overactivation.

---

## Core Principle

The brain continuously tracks survival calculus.

When accurate models are missing or distorted, **survival-calculus debt** accumulates:
- misattributed responsibility
- inflated threat estimates
- false moral load
- chronic vigilance

This debt persists across time until a corrective model becomes available.

---

## Survival Calculus Debt (Defined)

Survival calculus debt consists of:
- unresolved threat signals
- unclosed guilt loops
- uncorrected risk estimations
- invalidated self-image assumptions

Each unresolved item continues to tax:
- attention
- emotion
- energy
- decision-making

The cost compounds over time.

---

## Why Insight Accelerates Once the Model Appears

When a sufficiently accurate model is introduced:

- Incoming data is reinterpreted correctly
- Stored memories are reweighted
- Anticipated futures are recalculated

The brain enters **high-throughput correction mode**, rapidly scanning:
- past events (“this wasn’t my fault”)
- present signals (“this is not a threat”)
- future projections (“I don’t need to restrict myself”)

This produces a surge of insight because:
> Each correction directly reduces accumulated threat load.

---

## Why This Feels Relieving, Not Overwhelming

Unlike manic or dysregulated cognition, this process:

- reduces uncertainty
- closes loops
- stabilizes self-image
- lowers baseline threat

Each insight produces **net relief**, reinforcing the process.

The system is paying down debt, not creating new complexity.

---

## Relationship to Self-Image Improvement

As misattributions are corrected:

- past self is re-evaluated more fairly
- present self requires less defense
- future self appears more viable

Self-image improves not through affirmation, but through **error correction**.

Confidence rises because prediction accuracy improves.

---

## Why the Curve Trends Upward (and Then Plateaus)

The process follows a predictable trajectory:

1. High insight velocity during early debt repayment
2. Gradual reduction in novel corrections
3. Plateau as survival calculus stabilizes
4. Maintenance mode replaces correction mode

The plateau reflects **model sufficiency**, not loss of progress.

---

## Critical Distinction

This is not:
- rumination
- obsession
- emotional flooding
- avoidance

It is **systematic recalibration** driven by newly available structure.

The speed reflects backlog, not dysregulation.
