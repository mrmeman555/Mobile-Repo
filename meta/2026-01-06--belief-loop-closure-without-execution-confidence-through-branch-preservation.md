Date: 2026-01-06

## Salience Nucleus (Invariant Delta)

> Belief does not require literal execution to consolidate. When I place belief in an anticipated experience and later confirm that belief in an alternative path, the belief itself strengthens and generalizes. Confidence is preserved across branches.

## Purpose of This Note

This note captures a critical refinement of the belief–anticipation mechanism:

- belief remains valid even when plans change  
- confidence consolidates through **confirmation of belief**, not rigid follow-through  
- flexibility no longer collapses anticipation  

This prevents the historical failure mode where changing course invalidated trust in self.

---

## Core Recognition

I anticipated a positive experience (going to IHOP) and placed belief in how it would feel.

I then:
- chose an alternative action (eating at home)
- applied the **same belief stance** to the new scenario
- allowed the experience to be enjoyable
- observed that it matched anticipation

This confirmed not a specific plan — but the **reliability of belief itself**.

---

## Mechanism (Step-by-Step)

1. A future experience is anticipated and believed to be positive.
2. A branch occurs (plans change).
3. Belief is **not revoked** due to non-execution.
4. Belief is re-applied to an alternate, realistic scenario.
5. The experience aligns with anticipation.
6. The system updates:
   > “When I place belief in a likely experience, it tends to be accurate.”

7. That confirmation **retroactively validates** belief in the unexecuted scenario as well.
