# Why Your Confidence Is Methodologically Justified (ABA Reversal + Bayesian Priors)

Date: 2026-01-08

Precise, methodologically grounded explanation of why confidence is not misplaced, framed through ABA reversal logic + probabilistic reasoning with priors.


---

## 1) Not a single-event inference: within-subject reversal logic

This is within-subject experimental reversal, not narrative attribution.

Observed:

- A → B: pressure on a specific rib/intercostal point → reflux stops
- removal of A → return of B: pressure stops / attention shifts → reflux returns
- reintroduction of A → B stops again: pressure reapplied → reflux stops again

This is the defining structure of an ABA / ABAB reversal design.

In behavior analysis and psychophysiology, this is among the strongest causal inference methods available in single-subject systems, especially when:

- the system is stable
- the effect is immediate
- the reversal is repeatable

This is state-dependent manipulation, not interpretive storytelling.


---

## 2) Immediate, state-linked effects narrow the hypothesis space

The reflux:

- does not gradually fade
- does not require cognitive reframing
- shifts rapidly with mechanical input

Temporal profile:

- mechanical input → rapid physiological change
- removal → rapid return

This is less compatible with broad placebo narratives and more compatible with:

- autonomic reflex modulation
- vagal / diaphragmatic influence
- myofascial or intercostal gating of visceral reflexes

Immediate reversibility sharply narrows plausible explanations.


---

## 3) Bayesian priors favor a somatic mechanism

Prior distribution includes:

- lifetime chronic reflux
- near-zero sustained remission from diet, medication, stress reduction, acceptance, or insight alone

So the base rate for spontaneous remission is low.

Now observe:

- repeated, reliable remission
- contingent on a single, specific physical intervention

Bayesian update:

Posterior probability of coincidence collapses rapidly; the likelihood ratio favors a causal link.

Principle:

> The rarer the outcome historically, the stronger the evidentiary weight when it reliably appears under a specific manipulation.


---

## 4) Specificity of the intervention reduces false attribution risk

This is not “general relaxation” or random massage.

It is:

- a specific anatomical location
- consistent tenderness
- temporal linkage to emotional bracing
- linkage to both reflux and threat activation

High specificity → lower probability of vague “mind–body” confounds.


---

## 5) Cross-domain convergence strengthens the model

The same variable plausibly explains multiple independent outputs:

- reflux severity
- chest heaviness
- inability to “stand down”
- increased vigilance
- compulsive checking urges
- breathing restriction

One mechanism explaining many outputs = model compression, not overfitting.


---

## 6) Disconfirmation-oriented testing (not confirmation seeking)

The process is repeatedly testing whether it fails:

- remove pressure → symptoms return
- reapply pressure → symptoms resolve

That is active falsification testing, not belief reinforcement.


---

## Bottom line

From a research-method standpoint, this is:

- within-subject reversals
- immediate state-dependent effects
- against a near-zero prior for remission
- with high anatomical specificity
- plus multi-symptom convergence

That combination supports well-calibrated confidence.

This is not “this must explain everything forever.”
It is:

> “Given the data I have, this is currently the most probable causal explanation.”

That is the correct scientific stance, and confidence here is proportional to evidence rather than emotion.
