## What I Actually Achieved

I identified and operationalized a foundational control layer in human psychology that mainstream frameworks have repeatedly circled but failed to place cleanly:

The self as an embedding model that:

- continuously predicts, evaluates, and stabilizes identity,
- assigns authority among competing internal models,
- and determines which interpretations of reality are permitted to shape experience.

From that foundation, I derived and unified mechanisms across domains that are usually treated as unrelated.

## Core Contributions (Non-Exhaustive)

### 1) A Unifying Model of Shame Regulation

Shame as a self-stabilization mechanism, not merely an emotion.

Clear differentiation between:

- compassionate self-stabilization (open, learning-preserving),
- and narcissistic / scapegoating stabilization (closed, dominance-based).

Explains OCD, addiction, DP/DR, depression, and narcissistic dynamics through the same core process.

### 2) A Functional Account of Dominance, Authority, and Choice

Dominance reframed as authority assignment through choice, not aggression.

Explains why reclaiming authority over self-definition:

- collapses dissociation,
- restores motivation,
- and ends compulsive self-regulation.

Introduces compassionate dominance as a legitimate and necessary psychological function.

### 3) A Novel Reconsolidation Method

Reconsolidation driven by:

- chosen belief,
- refusal to grant epistemic authority to invalid self-models,
- and deliberate stance-taking under uncertainty.

Not symptom management — identity-level rewiring.

Predictably alters how memories are encoded, retrieved, and emotionally weighted.

### 4) A Functional Model of Narcissism

Narcissism explained as shame regulation via externalized dominance, not character pathology.

Accounts for:

- abuse cycles,
- moral disengagement,
- scapegoating behavior,
- and why compassion is structurally incompatible with narcissistic stabilization.

Reframes narcissism as a strategy, not a trait — which opens the door to intervention.

### 5) Bottom-Up Regulation via Eye Gaze

Identification and use of eye-gaze–based bottom-up interference to:

- disrupt dissociation,
- regulate autonomic state,
- and enable reconsolidation when top-down methods fail.

Functionally aligns with EMDR while extending beyond it through intentional integration with self-model work.
