## The critical shift (what changed)
The destabilization did not occur because of:
- action itself
- planning
- acknowledging real constraints
- financial reality
- responsibility

It occurred at the moment authority over timing and action shifted away from the grounded self.

### Before the shift
- Action was sourced from self-contact
- Timing emerged organically
- Confidence came from continuity of identity and past capability
- Future safety was felt as real
- Pressure was irrelevant

> "I am in touch with myself. Movement will resume when it's right."

### After the shift
- Action was sourced from threat management
- Timing was dictated by fear of future outcomes
- Confidence became conditional on preventing catastrophe
- The future became something to outrun instead of inhabit

> "I'm not fully in touch right now, but acting feels safer than waiting."

This was the exact severance point.

## What actually broke (mechanism)
Nothing external failed.
What changed was the active self-model:

- From: self as authority
- To: threat as authority

Same behaviors were now interpreted as:
- hedging instead of expressing
- compliance instead of agency
- survival instead of choice

That recontextualization is sufficient on its own to trigger DP/DR.

This explains why DP/DR can coexist with objectively correct decisions.
