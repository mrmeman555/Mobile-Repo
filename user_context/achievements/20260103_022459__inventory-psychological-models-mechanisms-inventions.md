# Inventory: Psychological Models / Mechanisms / “Inventions” (Reference List)

## I. Memory, Belief, and Identity Re-Encoding Models

### 1. Chosen Belief–Mediated Memory Reconsolidation
**Core insight**:  
Memory reconsolidation is mediated by what context is weighted as self-relevant while the memory is active, not by erasing or disputing the memory.

**Mechanism**:
- Voluntary retrieval of memory
- Simultaneous maintenance of a chosen belief / stance
- Defiant refusal to let shame be the dominant tag
- Contextual information (past, present, future self) is weighted intentionally
- Memory re-encodes around the held self-image, not the original shame

**Key distinction**:  
This is not cognitive disputation. It is context weighting during reconsolidation.

---

### 2. Defiance-as-Stabilizer Model
**Core insight**:  
Defiance (non-reactive, non-aggressive) functions as a stabilizing force that prevents shame from closing the reconsolidation loop.

**Mechanism**:
- Shame arises → treated as allowed signal, not threat
- Defiance holds the line: “This does not define me.”
- Prevents collapse, submission, or self-abandonment
- Enables memory to remain plastic long enough to update

---

### 3. Self-Image as an Upstream Variable
**Core insight**:  
Self-image must be upstream of perception, emotion, motivation, and memory—never downstream of performance.

**Contrast**:
- Old model: “I act → outcome → self-evaluation”
- New model: “I am → therefore action and outcome follow”

This shift explains:
- Motivation returning
- Grayness lifting
- Ability to experience success before acting

---

### 4. Future-Self Experiential Bridging
**Core insight**:  
The brain can experience future outcomes in the present when identity stability is secured.

**Mechanism**:
- Chosen belief about identity (“I am capable / safe / effective”)
- Future outcome becomes experientially accessible
- Action cost drops dramatically
- Motivation emerges naturally

This explains the “this will happen” felt certainty you experienced.

## II. Loving-Kindness Reframed as a Technical Process

### 5. Loving-Kindness as Externalized Stable Presence
**Core insight**:  
Loving-kindness is not emotion generation — it is maintaining awareness of a stable, approving presence in perceptual proximity.

**Key properties**:
- Treated as external to the wounded self
- Spatially proximal (felt as “there with me”)
- Non-contingent approval
- Persists regardless of internal state

This is why it:
- Produces shaking, blinking, autonomic release
- Stabilizes identity during threat
- Enables memory processing without collapse

---

### 6. Presence Permanence Principle
**Core insight**:  
Healing depends on believing the presence cannot be lost, regardless of thoughts, emotions, or actions.

**Critical phrase**:
> “Even when I don’t remember it, it is still there.”

This counters:
- Moral contingency
- Fear of abandonment
- Compulsive self-monitoring

---

### 7. Dual-Stream Awareness Model
**Core insight**:  
Healing occurs when multiple streams are allowed simultaneously:

- Stream A: Pain / shame / threat
- Stream B: Stable loving presence
- Stream C: Volitional stance / chosen belief

No stream is required to dominate.

---

### 8. Loving-Kindness vs. Fierce Self-Compassion Distinction
You clarified this precisely:

**Fierce self-compassion**
- Boundary-setting
- Rejection of false narratives
- Closing off harmful interpretations

**Soft loving-kindness**

Healing requires both, applied deliberately.
